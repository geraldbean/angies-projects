Centrifugal Lens

An idea to keep drone cameras clear

Project Overview

Over the holidays I watched a National Geographic Documentary on Cheetahs where they discussed filming in the rain being a challenge for the camera lens. They had this device called a centrifugal lens that would go overtop their camera lens and begin to spin. This would generate a centrifugal force and clear the water droplets from the camera lens. I figure another place this could be applicable was on a drone camera in rainy conditions or even smoggy ones when they are used to fight forest fires.

My Design


I attempted to create a simple CAD model for a centrifugal lens spinner of my own. I modelled a circular mold to fit around a glass lens. Additionally I added small holes through the rim to account for suction that could occur or fog that could collect on the actual camera lens. I think this kind of design could be implimented on smaller drone cameras that are used to survey areas in unideal conditions.
